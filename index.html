I earned my PhD in Linguistics from the Department of Linguistics and Philosophy at MIT on September 19, 2018. My research interest lies in understanding Quantifier Scope ambiguities and its manifestations, particularly in scope rigid languages like Bangla (Bengali). So far I have explored Scrambling, Additive particles and Distributive numerals. My PhD Dissertation is on Distributive numerals. I have worked on Correlative constructions as well. At present, I am working on Indo-Aryan Conditionals. Recently I gave a talk on the difference between Participial and Correlative conditionals in Bangla.

Since September 2019, I have worked as an ad-hoc assistant professor and visiting scholar at the Department of Linguistics, at the University of Delhi. In the Fall semester of 2019, I taught Phonology-I to MA Ist semester students and I also taught Semantics-I to MA IIIrd semester students.

Previously I worked as a lecturer at the School of Languages and Linguistics at Jadavpur University in Kolkata, India. In the Spring semester of 2019, I taught a course on Philosophy of Language, which is a mandatory course for MA in Linguistics in Jadavpur University.

I was also part of the UGC-SRIELI project, being appointed as a Research Associate at the project center at School of Languages and Linguistics, Jadavpur University. The UGC project is designed to document endangered and indigenous languages of India; the center at Jadavpur University is focused on endangered and lesser known languages of West Bengal and works on building dictionaries and grammars of each of these languages. The center has already digitally archived linguistic and related ethnographic data.
